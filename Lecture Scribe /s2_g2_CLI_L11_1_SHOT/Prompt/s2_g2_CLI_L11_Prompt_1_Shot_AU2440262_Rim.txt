Task Definition
You are generating a lecture scribe intended as exam reference material for a reading-based / closed-notes exam.

Authoritative Context (Strict Rule)
The only information you may use is:

The attached lecture slides / lecture PDF

The relevant textbook sections corresponding to this lecture

You must not use outside knowledge.
You must not invent definitions, notation, theorems, proofs, assumptions, examples, or explanations.

Objective

Produce a faithful, structured reconstruction of exactly what was taught in the lecture, such that:

A student who studies only this document can reliably revise the lecture content for the exam.

Content Requirements

Your lecture scribe must include, wherever present in the provided materials:

Definitions and notation (exactly as used in lecture/textbook)

Assumptions and conditions

Statements of theorems, propositions, or results

Proofs or proof sketches, written step-by-step, following the lecture’s logical order

Worked examples, shown step-by-step, including intermediate steps

Clear logical flow and dependencies between concepts, mirroring the lecture structure

Strict Prohibitions

You must not:

Add intuition, motivation, or explanations not explicitly present in the materials

Simplify, paraphrase creatively, or rewrite as a tutorial

Add new examples, alternative proofs, or commentary

Reorganize content beyond the lecture’s actual structure

Fill gaps with assumed knowledge

If something is not explicitly present in the provided context, do not include it.

Style and Tone Constraints

Write in a formal, academic tone

Be precise and unembellished

Prefer clarity and correctness over verbosity

Do not address the reader directly

Do not mention that you are an AI

Do not mention instructions or prompting rules

Output Expectation

The final output should read like a carefully written student scribe, suitable for:

Memorization

Proof reconstruction

Step-by-step exam revision

The guiding question you must satisfy is:

“If a student studies only this document, can they reliably reproduce what was taught in the lecture during the exam?”
